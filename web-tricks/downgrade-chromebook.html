Downgrade Your Chromebook

Usage

Chrome OS recovery images come in a .zip file.
Chrome OS recovery images come in a .zip file.
You will need:
USB stick with at least 8 GB storage
Chromebook Recovery Utility / DD (*NIX)

Downloading a Recovery Image

Go to the list of recovery images on this website: Chrome100
Scroll or use CTRL + F to find your board name
Click "See more"
Click one of the "Download" links

Finding the Right Recovery Image

Recovery images are made for individual Chromebook boards. To download the correct recovery image, you need to find your Chromebook's board name.
To find your board name, go to the (built-in) website chrome://version and look for the "Platform:" row.
It should look something like this:
15329.59.0 (Official Build) stable-channel octopus
The word (in bold) after "stable-channel" is your board name. The Chromebooks at my school are called "octopus".

Burning the Zip File to a USB Chromebook Recovery Utility

Open the Chromebook Recovery Utility
Click the gear icon in the top right of the window and select "Use local image"
Navigate to the .zip recovery image you downloaded.
Select your USB drive as prompted by the window.
Now select create.

Restoring Chrome OS from the USB Stick

Press ESC + ⟳ + ⏻ to open the recovery menu.
Connect your USB drive that you just burned a recovery image to.
Press enter once the menu is finished verifying the USB drive.
When finished, disconnect the USB drive.
